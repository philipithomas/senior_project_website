--- layout: post title: Kill The Landline Now Open Source ---

Kill The Landline was our Startup Weekend Columbia 2012 project. The system targeted baby boomers that owned a cell phone, but still paid for a land telephone line because they had used it for decades. People still called a customer's old number, but their cell phone was their primary method of communication. However, maintaining a landline cost around $30/month.

Kill The Landline was built to keep an old landline number, but forward it to one or more cell phones. We priced it at $10/month with Tropo, and it would take roughly a week to transfer a number to our system.

While the pitch won an honorable mention at the event, the software was never completely finished, and we did not continue building a business. Hence, we decided to publish the code from Kill The Landline, both as an example of a Tropo application, and as an example of what a team of three can produce at Startup Weekend.
